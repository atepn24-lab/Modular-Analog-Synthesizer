# Modular Analog Synthesizer

This project explores the design and implementation of a voltage-controlled modular synthesizer. The system consists of several interconnected analog modules that generate, process, and shape audio signals through control voltages. The goal of the project was to study both the theoretical and practical aspects of analog sound synthesis and to investigate the challenges of translating simulated circuits into physical hardware.

The synthesizer is composed of six main modules: a Voltage-Controlled Oscillator (VCO), Voltage-Controlled Filter (VCF), Voltage-Controlled Amplifier (VCA), Mixer, Wavefolder, and Sequencer. Together, these modules form a complete signal chain capable of generating and modifying audio signals in real time.

Circuit design and analysis were performed using Multisim, while schematic capture and PCB design were carried out in EasyEDA. The project involved extensive work with operational amplifiers, transistors, resistors, capacitors, diodes, and through-hole components. PCB layouts for the VCO and Sequencer were developed using EasyEDA and manufactured using standard THT components.

Although all modules operated correctly in simulation, only the Sequencer and Wavefolder were successfully implemented in hardware. The remaining modules encountered practical issues related to component tolerances, noise sensitivity, power distribution, and circuit integration. These difficulties highlighted the difference between ideal simulations and real-world analog electronics.

Throughout the project, valuable experience was gained in analog circuit design, PCB development, soldering, debugging, oscilloscope measurements, and laboratory work. The project provided practical insight into signal processing, modular synthesis, and the behavior of real electronic systems.
